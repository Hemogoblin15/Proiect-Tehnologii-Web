ONLINE MEETINGS

As technology continues to reshape our lives, online meetings have become an integral part of our professional and personal interactions. This article aims to provide guidance and tips for newcomers on how to behave during an online meeting.

1. Prepare in Advance: Before the meeting, familiarize yourself with the meeting agenda and any relevant materials. Test your audio and video settings to ensure they are working properly. Join the meeting a few minutes early to avoid any last-minute technical glitches.

2. Dress Appropriately: Although you may be attending the meeting from the comfort of your home, it's essential to dress professionally. Dressing appropriately shows respect for the meeting and helps create a positive impression among other participants.

3. Choose a Suitable Location: Select a quiet and well-lit area for your online meeting. Ensure that the background is clean, organized, and free from distractions. Consider using a virtual background if you prefer not to share your actual surroundings.

4. Maintain Professionalism: Treat an online meeting as you would an in-person meeting. Be attentive, engaged, and maintain a professional demeanor. Avoid multitasking or engaging in unrelated activities during the meeting. Give your full attention to the discussion and actively participate when appropriate.

5. Mind Your Body Language Non-verbal cues play a significant role in communication, even in virtual meetings. Sit upright, maintain eye contact by looking at the camera, and use appropriate facial expressions to convey your engagement and interest. Avoid excessive fidgeting or distracting gestures.

6. Use Clear and Concise Communication: When speaking, enunciate your words clearly and speak at a moderate pace. Avoid interrupting others and wait for your turn to speak. Use the mute feature when not speaking to prevent background noise from disrupting the meeting.

7. Be Respectful and Courteous: Respect the opinions and contributions of others in the meeting. Wait for others to finish speaking before sharing your thoughts. Use inclusive language and avoid offensive or discriminatory remarks. Treat all participants with professionalism and courtesy.

8. Engage Actively: Participate in the meeting by asking relevant questions, providing input, and sharing your ideas when appropriate. Actively listen to others and show interest in their contributions. Take notes to help you remember important points and action items.

9. Be Mindful of Technology: Familiarize yourself with the meeting platform and its features. Learn how to use the chat function, raise your hand, or share your screen if necessary. Test your microphone and camera settings to ensure they are functioning correctly before the meeting starts.

10. Follow Meeting Etiquette: Adhere to the meeting guidelines set by the organizer. Avoid side conversations or off-topic discussions that may distract others. If you need to step away momentarily, inform the participants or use the "Leave Meeting" option temporarily.
